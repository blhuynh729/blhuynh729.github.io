# Sensor Categories

This section covers a variety of sensors designed to detect different aspects of the physical world, from environmental conditions to motion and human interaction. Sensors are organized into three primary categories, each with its own subcategories.

---

## [Environmental Sensing](Environmental-Sensing/index.md)

Sensors that measure conditions in the surrounding environment:

- **Air (Atmospheric)** — gas concentration, air quality, humidity
- **Light (Optical)** — brightness, color, UV/IR light
- **Sound (Acoustic)** — noise levels, audio signals
- **Water (Liquid & Soil)** — moisture, water quality, soil conditions

---

## [Motion and Position Sensing](Motion-and-position-sensing/index.md)

Sensors that track movement, location, or spatial orientation:

- **Proximity & Presence** — object detection, distance sensing
- **Motion Detection** — acceleration, vibration, movement
- **Orientation & Navigation** — tilt, rotation, heading (IMUs, gyros)

---

## [Human Sensing and Controls](Human-Sensing-and-Controls/index.md)

Sensors that capture user input or biological signals:

- **Inputs** — buttons, switches, keypads
- **Biometrics** — heart rate, oxygen levels
